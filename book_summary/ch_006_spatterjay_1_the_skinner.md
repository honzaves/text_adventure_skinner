# Chapter: Spatterjay 1 - The Skinner

## Events
Janer wakes with a bandaged, tattooed hand, learning he’s been infected with a virus offering both benefits (resistance to injury, increased strength) and risks (organ failure, fungal susceptibility). He collects a hornet specimen for research, guided by “The Mind” – an AI inhabiting his consciousness. Meanwhile, Keech, transporting a “package” for the Hive, deviates from his mission to seek medical attention at Coram after sustaining injuries. He’s ambushed by two Batians but defeats them with his pulse-gun and escapes on his scooter to the black stone island, “The Little Flint,” to initiate self-repair with a nanofactory unit. This process awakens long-dormant sensations. Sniper and Thirteen investigate a strangely behaving molly carp, discovering it’s a Warden-monitored predator with a micro-transponder. Janer and Erlin discuss the stagnant nature of their location and Erlin recounts a horrific incident from her early days aboard the *Treader*, involving a leech attack, the transformation of a crewmember (Peck) into the monstrous “Skinner,” and the gruesome process of re-skinning him.

## Characters Present
- **Janer Cord Anders:** Recently infected with the virus, curious about its effects, and collecting samples. He’s arrogant and probing.
- **The Mind:** An AI or entity residing within Janer's consciousness, providing information about the virus, and possessing a detached, analytical perspective.
- **Keech:** Skilled fighter, biomechanical enhancements, cynical, determined to repair himself despite severe injuries. He references a prayer to “Anubis Arisen.”
- **Hive Mind:** A collective intelligence communicating with Keech, concerned about the package and Keech's change of plans.
- **Batians (two):** Armed individuals ambushing Keech, seemingly expecting an easy target.
- **Erlin:** Experienced, troubled, and initially reluctant to share her past. She is haunted by what she witnessed and seems somewhat detached. She is knowledgeable about the local ecology and the effects of the virus.
- **Captain Ron:** Old, experienced, terse, fought in the Prador war.
- **Shib, Svan, Tors, Dime:** Crew members dealing with the aftermath of the ambush.
- **Sniper & Thirteen:** War drones investigating the molly carp, pragmatic and bureaucratic.
- **Roach:** Cynical observer on the ship.
- **Peck, Ambel, Anne, Pland:** Crew members involved in the incident with the leech and the Skinner.

## Locations
- **Ocean/Seabed:** Described with disturbed silt, broken shells, and a deep oceanic trench.
- **Ship Deck/Interior:** Where much of the dialogue and observation takes place.
- **Janer’s Bunk:** Where Janer wakes up and discusses the virus with the Mind.
- **Scooter/Cleanser:** A vehicle used by Keech.
- **Dingle at the head of the beach:** Where Keech is ambushed.
- **Coram:** A location with medical facilities, where Keech intends to go.
- **Dome:** A location Keech considered, and where Erlin was transported from.
- **“The Little Flint”:** A small, sloping black stone island used by Keech for repairs.
- **Skinner’s Islands:** Home to the Skinner creature.
- **Coastal shallows:** Where rhinoworms live.

## Creatures & Organisms
- **Monstrous Whelk:** A large, thick-skinned creature with tentacles, preyed upon by a heirodont.
- **Deepwater Flesh-Eating Heirodont:** A predator hunting the whelk.
- **Filter-Worms:** Large worms living under boulders, prey for the whelk.
- **Leeches:** Giant leeches with "trumpet mouths."
- **Hornets:** Used as subjects for research related to the virus and as message carriers.
- **Molly Carp:** Small drone-like creature, exhibits unusual speed and endurance, has a peanut-sized brain, and is part of the Warden’s study. It is a class-three intelligence and a predator.
- **Whelks:** Creatures being surveyed by Sniper and Thirteen.
- **Rhinoworms:** Live in coastal shallows and are a source of meat.
- **Skinner:** A horrifying creature that was once human, now obsessed with skin. Dangerous and aggressive.
- **Batians:** Humanoid ambushers.

- **Laser Carbine:** A weapon carried by the female Batian.
- **Brushed Aluminium Cylinders:** Used to collect and contain specimens (the hornet).

- **Aug:** A device providing visual input to Keech, malfunctioning during the ambush.
- **Scooter Comunit Signal Bell:** Used for communication.

- **Micro-transponder:** Implanted in the carp, linked to its nerve ganglia, and monitored by the Warden.
- **Ultrasound scanner:** Used by Sniper to examine the carp.
- **Surgical laser:** Carried by Erlin, modified to remove safety limiters.
- **Blunderbuss:** Used by Ambel.

## Lore & World Details
- A virus exists that affects humans differently than insects, requiring a large infusion to take hold in humans.
- “Hoopers” are individuals infected with the virus, identified by a “mark” (a blue tattoo).
- “Minds” are entities that can exist independently of a physical body.
- There is a “shuttle port” and a “Dome” suggesting space travel or enclosed settlements.
- **Prador War:** A past conflict.
- **Polity:** A governing body with a Warden and surveillance technology.
- **Heavy-world reification:** A type of biomechanical being with strengthened bones.
- Humans who do not consume Earth food cease to be human.
- Earth food keeps the virus "in abeyance."

## Tone & Atmosphere
The chapter is a blend of unsettling biological detail, tense action, and creeping dread. The descriptions of the creatures, the virus, and the Skinner are grotesque and alien. The atmosphere is gritty and cynical, with a sense of weariness and a long-running conflict. There’s a growing feeling of paranoia and a sense that the characters are trapped in a stagnant, decaying world.
