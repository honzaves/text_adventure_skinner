# Chapter: Spatterjay 1 - The Skinner

## Events
A glister enjoyed a meal before being consumed by a larger creature, mirroring the crew of the *Batians* discarding a deceased comrade overboard. Frisk, approaching Keech’s location, intends to remain undetected while observing Captain Drum, who is now controlled by a “spider thrall” under the influence of Jay Hoop. Keech awoke from a procedure with heightened senses, revealing his mission to kill Jay Hoop and discussing the rare poison “Sprine” harvested from leeches by Hoopers. He aggressively questioned Erlin about Hoop, then suffered a breakdown as his cybernetic implants clashed with his organic brain.

Keech was restrained after this episode, while Drum underwent a disturbing procedure involving a Prador creature and a metallic implant. Frisk, agitated and believing Drum revealed information about Keech, attacked Drum and the ship’s sail with a pulse-gun. Meanwhile, Ambel and the crew meticulously separated components from leech bile using a complex setup, while autonomous scanners SM12 and SM13 searched for Rebecca Frisk, reporting to the Warden. 

Olian Tay observed three ships approaching, anticipating a visit from Captain Sprage. A sail, *Windcheater*, abducted Tay, carrying her towards the ships, where Sprage questioned her about Frisk and agreed to let her join the hunt. On the *Ahab*, Peck confessed to feeding something that “called” to him, just as Captain Ron arrived. Ron, Erlin, and other off-worlders—including Janer Cord Anders and Sable Keech—boarded the *Treader*. Keech immediately attacked Ambel, claiming to be Sable Keech from a historical mission to free slaves on Hoop and believing Ambel is actually Gosk Balem. Ron denied this, restraining Keech, who then fixated on a mysterious sea chest.

## Tone & Atmosphere
The narrative is gritty, violent, and filled with a sense of unease and hidden agendas. There’s a clinical detachment in the descriptions of bodily functions and violence, combined with a growing sense of mystery surrounding the characters’ pasts and the true nature of their mission. The atmosphere is tense and desperate, hinting at a long-running conflict and the potential for catastrophic failure.
