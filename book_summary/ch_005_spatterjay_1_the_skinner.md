# Chapter: Spatterjay 1 - The Skinner

## Events
Four glisters hunt turbul, efficiently killing them despite not needing all the meat. Meanwhile, Sniper and Thirteen (SM13) survey a horseshoe-shaped islet, discovering a sea cave filled with hammer-whelk shells containing valuable amberclam pearls, fossilized glister, and green sapphire. Thirteen reveals she illegally modified herself to excavate the cave and transport the materials, negotiating a 50/50 split with Sniper. They are attacked by a molly carp, which Sniper disables with an EM pulse after narrowly avoiding being killed.

Keech, a reified individual on a seven-hundred-year mission, spends time at the Baitman bar drinking sea-cane rum and reflecting on past events, including the takedown of Corbel Frane. He contemplates his future and seeks information about Jay Hoop (the Skinner) from an Old Captain named Sprage. A malfunction in his preservative systems, triggered by the Spatterjay virus contracted at a museum, forces him to use a cleansing unit, drawing unwanted attention. He is asked to leave the bar and secures transport to the shuttle port, continuing to battle the virus.

At the shuttle port, Keech picks up a scan-proof hexagonal container from a Golem android, Paul A2-18, keyed to Janer’s DNA. He is then ambushed by five Batians mercenaries, escaping on a hover scooter and attracting the attention of the Warden (an AI), resulting in a fine for violating flying regulations. Collapsing on a beach, Keech’s Intertox system fails, causing hallucinations of those he has killed and forcing him to consider his options: full AI conversion or using a lozenge from a lifecoven woman. He is confronted by hallucinatory figures – Frane, Cojan, and Rimsc – before realizing he is truly alone and receiving error messages about a malfunctioning cerebral probe.

Interwoven with Keech’s journey, a Prador named Frisk reflects on her past enjoyment of inflicting suffering on force-grown humans, particularly with her companion Jay. She expresses frustration with a durable, unreactive Hooper she has been abusing and anticipates finding more suitable subjects in the space she is entering. She crushes a louse while preparing to leave, lamenting her waning ability to find pleasure in malice. On a ship, the crew extracts bile from a duct, a dangerous task with a cautionary tale attached, while a sail detects an island five kilometers north.

## Lore & World Details
- **Polity AI Technology:** Sniper is a pinnacle of this technology.
- **Warden:** Governing force prohibiting drone modifications.
- **Subsumption:** Fate for drones breaking the Warden’s rules.
- **Hoopers:** A group of people, some of whom are gem dealers.
- **Reified Beings:** Brought back to a functional state after death, like Keech.
- **Prador/Human War:** A past conflict between the Prador and humans.
- **Force-grown Humans:** Bred for consumption.
- **Yanwood:** Durable wood used in ship construction.
- **The Eight:** Employed the Batians mercenaries in the past.

## Tone & Atmosphere
The overall tone is detached, melancholic, and often grim. The hunting scenes are clinical, while Keech’s journey is marked by weariness, urgency, and a haunted past. Frisk’s sections are disturbing and cold, focusing on sadism and the loss of pleasure. The world is presented as technologically advanced but gritty, with a long and often violent history.
