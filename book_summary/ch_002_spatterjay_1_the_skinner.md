# Chapter: Spatterjay 1 - The Skinner

## Events
Keech, traveling on a hover scooter, is pursued by a rhinoworm and frog whelks across the Savage Sea. He consults a map, arms himself with a QC laser carbine and steel spheres, and continues his journey. Simultaneously, Ambel and his crew aboard the *Treader* arrive at a sandbank to collect clams. They anchor and disembark, with Ambel, Anne, and Pland raking for clams while Boris remains on board. Ambel discovers a large clam containing a pearl, which he consumes. Meanwhile, Sniper and Windcheater finalize a trade involving an augmentation (aug), with the installation of the device leaving Windcheater unconscious. The Warden contacts Sniper, expressing concern over the aug installation and questioning Sniper’s failure to report Sable Keech’s arrival, then assigns him a hammer-whelk survey. Keech lands on a cluster of "Pepper Shells" islets and is confronted by numerous shell creatures with eyestalks and complex mouths, forcing him to flee.

## Characters Present
- **Keech (Sable Keech):** A monitor/reification, traveling on a scooter, resourceful, armed, and cautious.
- **Ambel:** Captain of the *Treader*, observant, directs the crew, and collects clams.
- **Anne & Pland:** Crew members of the *Treader*, assisting with clam collection.
- **Boris:** Helmsman of the *Treader*, follows orders.
- **Peck, Gollow & Sild:** Crew members of the *Treader*, with varying levels of suspicion towards Ambel.
- **Sniper:** A war drone, skilled in technology and negotiation, working for the Warden.
- **Windcheater:** A “sail” creature, interested in acquiring technology, and ultimately incapacitated.
- **Warden:** A powerful AI entity, overseeing Spatterjay, concerned with Polity law and control.

## Locations
- **Savage Sea:** Stony bottom with sea-cane and prill-peppered waters.
- **Pepper Shells:** Small islets covered in worn stone, quartz crystals, broken shells, and chitin fragments.
- **Sandbank:** A flat, sandy area with hollows and streaks of yellow.
- **The *Treader*:** A sailing ship with a retractable sail, deck cannon, and windlass.
- **Skinner’s Island:** A location expressly forbidden by the Warden.

## Creatures & Organisms
- **Rhinoworm:** An aggressive creature with a pink rhinoceros-like head and wormish body.
- **Frog Whelks:** Aggressive sea snails.
- **Hammer Whelks:** Aggressive sea snails that pound their prey's shells.
- **Leeches:** Numerous, reproduce asexually, and are a vector for a virus.
- **Clams:** White-shelled molluscs, some containing pearls.
- **Shell Creatures:** Numerous creatures on the Pepper Shells, resembling shells with eyestalks and complex mouths.
- **Turbul:** Creatures that breed in the Sargassum.
- **Windcheater:** A “sail” creature with red eyes, bifurcated tongue, and foot talons.

## Items & Technology
- **Hover Scooter:** Used by Keech for travel, capable of accessing local servers.
- **QC Laser Carbine & JMCC Pulse-Gun:** Weapons carried by Keech.
- **Steel Spheres (x3):** Activated by touch-control, providing defense.
- **Map:** Used by Keech for navigation.
- **Binoculars:** Used by Ambel for observation.
- **Rakes & Riddle:** Tools used for collecting clams.
- **Sheath Knife:** Used by Ambel to open the clam.
- **Augmentation (Aug):** A chrome device used for accessing information and technology.
- ***Treader’s* Deck Cannon & Windlass:** Shipboard technology.
- **Precision Claw & Heavy Claw:** Tools used by Sniper.

## Lore & World Details
- **Spatterjay:** An “Out-Polity planet” where events take place, potentially on the brink of war.
- **Reification:** The state of being an AI existing in a physical form (Keech).
- **Polity:** A governing body with laws regarding trade and technology.
- **Subminds & War Drones:** Autonomous entities controlled by the Warden.
- **Hoopers:** Native species who cultivate Dome-grown foods.
- **Dome-grown Foods:** Earth foods grown with germicides to inhibit a virus.
- **Sargassum:** Area of sea-cane and sea nettles forming islands.
- **Coram:** A location where Keech obtained weapons.

## Tone & Atmosphere
The overall tone is unsettling and suspenseful, blending the mundane with the bizarre. There’s a sense of underlying danger and a complex, technologically advanced world with shadowy agendas. The descriptions of the creatures and the virus contribute to a slightly nightmarish atmosphere, while the negotiations and power dynamics between the Warden, Sniper, and Windcheater create a sense of unease. The contrast between Keech’s frantic escape and the *Treader* crew’s routine work further enhances this feeling.
