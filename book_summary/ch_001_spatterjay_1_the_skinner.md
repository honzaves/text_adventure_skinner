# Chapter: Spatterjay 1 - The Skinner

## Events
Ambel and his crew efficiently harvest turbul, stripping the creatures to their skeletons which swim away, and begin pickling the meat. Simultaneously, Janer, Keech, and Erlin cross the glassite line separating Polity and Spatterjay, observing the stark contrast between cultivated fields and decaying remains. They question a Warden AI about border control and enter a Spatterjay town, purchasing weapons after a lengthy negotiation. Ambel tends to a grotesquely mutated head, “the Skinner,” in his cabin. The crew shifts focus from turbul to amberclams due to a night moon. Janer is contacted by a Hive mind, tasked with finding Erlin’s Captain, and witnesses a brutal bare-knuckle fight, winning a bet on Domby. A subsequent attempt to collect his winnings leads to a confrontation, resolved by Keech’s intervention with a pulse-gun. Janer receives his winnings, is aided by Captain Ron when the tout attempts to reclaim the money, and learns of a man needing finger reattachment. They enter the Baitman, a Hooper drinking den, where Keech reveals his cybernetic augmentations. Erlin arrives, carefully lifted by Captain Ron, and asks about Ambel’s location – the Sargassum. The evening ends with Janer buying drinks for the bar, a blurred recollection of conversation about Jay Hoop, being carried by Ron, and ultimately losing consciousness after vomiting into the sea.

## Lore & World Details
- The “Polity” and “Spatterjay” are distinct territories.
- The Hoopers do not enforce border controls.
- Turbul is harvested for its meat, which is pickled.
- Polity technology is slowly integrating into everyday life.
- Spatterjay is a dangerous place with a history tied to Jay Hoop.
- The virus affects people differently.
- Ship oaths are a form of binding agreement.
- Reification is a concept related to Erlin's Captain.
- The environment of Spatterjay is hostile to insects due to “filaments.”

## Tone & Atmosphere
The narrative is gritty, practical, and unsettling. It blends the routine of a life at sea with the harsh realities of a dangerous alien world. There’s a sense of underlying mystery, a detached observation of violence, and a growing feeling of unease as Janer becomes entangled in the affairs of Spatterjay and the Hive mind. The atmosphere is often bleak, with descriptions of decay and mutation juxtaposed with moments of pragmatic efficiency.
