# Chapter: Spatterjay 1 - The Skinner

## Events
A giant leech died after being poisoned by its own bile, attracting a feeding frenzy of various oceanic creatures – boxies, frog whelks, hammer whelks, turbul, and glisters – near an atoll. Simultaneously, a Prador ship was destroyed by an unknown weapon and crashed onto a rocky island “dingle.” Janer activated a device using a red crystal and a hexagonal box, seemingly sending the crystal away. Ambel beheaded a creature called the Skinner after it was stung by hornets, presenting its head to Captain Ron, while a group of Hoopers built a pyre to burn the remains. Keech awoke injured to find Frisk dead, killed during a struggle. 

Keech observed a group surrounding a bound crewman, Forlam, exhibiting strange behavior. Drum, an Old Captain, attempted to kill Rebecca Frisk, now inhabiting a Hooper body, but was stopped by Olian Tay and Captain Sprage, who decided to imprison her for millennia in a metallic coffin. Thirteen, a drone, investigated the coffin, witnessing Frisk’s desperate thrashing. 

Ambel defended himself against accusations of being Gosk Balem, claiming amnesia, while Janer received a substantial sum of money via a Hive link. Sprage revealed he knew Ambel was Gosk Balem, the one who sabotaged a reactor to signal for ECS rescue, and had deliberately kept it secret. A Prador, Vrell, arrived on the beach, intending to repair his father’s ship, but chose not to take Frisk from the coffin, while a molly carp began to follow him. The Warden revealed it intentionally allowed a war drone, Sniper, to remain independent.

- **The Skinner:** A creature with remnants of human thought, driven by hunger and hatred.

## Lore & World Details
- **Sprine:** A poison affecting creatures in the ocean.
- **Hoopers:** A species that practices “coring.”
- **The Warden:** A controlling entity maintaining “balance.”
- **The Polity:** A faction fearing the development of this place.
- **Hive:** A location or organization.
- **Prador:** A species with advanced technology.
- **ECS:** The entity for whom Ambel created the beacon.
- **Coring:** A process involving the removal of brains and spinal columns.
- **Irradiated water:** The water around the island is contaminated.

## Tone & Atmosphere
The overall tone is dark, violent, and unsettling, filled with gruesome deaths, predatory behavior, and long-held secrets. There’s a sense of mystery surrounding the motivations of the Warden, the true nature of the events unfolding, and the complex relationships between the characters. A fragile peace is established, but hints of larger conflicts and hidden agendas remain, creating a tense and ambiguous atmosphere.
