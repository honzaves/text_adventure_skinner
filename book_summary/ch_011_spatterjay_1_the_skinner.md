# Chapter: Spatterjay 1 - The Skinner

## Chapter Summary

## Events
The chapter opens with a chaotic scene of predation on Spatterjay 1, as Glisters feeding on a Turbul are attacked by a tentacled predator. This draws the attention of a Heirodont and the Warden AI, who dispatches a “submind ghost” for observation. Simultaneously, negotiations between the Prador and humans/AI are disrupted by internal Prador conflict, escalating into a railgun attack and widespread destruction. 

On the ship, Frisk amuses herself with dangerous games, shooting leeches and capturing a frog whelk that injures a mercenary. Meanwhile, Erlin treats Captain Ambel’s wounds, noting a strange discoloration, before Ron and Forlam bind him under parole. Keech escapes confinement, only to confront a creature emerging from a sea-chest. 

The crew discovers Ambel is actually Gosk Balem, a former slave and slave master with a horrific past. He recounts being harpooned, surviving with a leech mouth, and being rescued by Captain Sprage. Keech confirms Ambel’s Hooper physiology, making him nearly unkillable due to the Spatterjay virus, and declares a sentence upon him has been carried out. 

The chapter culminates with the discovery that “blanks” – humans with altered minds – are being collected and moved into cold storage on heavy-lifters, their DNA sampled by AI drones. The Warden AI observes these events, transmitting information back to Spatterjay, while the ship encounters a molly carp and is attacked by prill and a giant leech, resulting in Tors’ death and a desperate attempt to activate the ship’s augmentation generator. Janer, suspicious of the AI’s motives, discards a Hive link.

## Locations
**Primary:** A ship (various decks, cabins, motor room), Prador world (cracked hardpan), Spatterjay 1 (seabottom), Prador home world (heavy-lifter docks, cold storage), Treader (another ship), Skinner’s Island.

## Creatures & Organisms
The chapter features a diverse range of creatures, including the predatory Glisters and Heirodont, the dangerous tentacled predator, the aggressive frog whelk, the monstrous Skinner, and the resilient Hooper (Ambel). The presence of leeches, prill, and a molly carp add to the dangerous environment. Windcheater is a unique sail creature capable of speech and negotiation.

## Items & Technology
Significant technology includes the Warden’s “submind ghost” and associated communication systems (Runcible, AI ships, virtual fingernails), the Prador railgun and ECS monitors, Frisk’s pulse-gun, the ship’s augmentation generator (AG), and the AI drones collecting DNA samples. Contracts, harpoons, and cell-welders are also utilized. The Hive link is a device used for communication with the Warden.

## Lore & World Details
The chapter reveals a complex history of conflict and uneasy alliances between humans, AI, and the Prador. The Spatterjay virus is a key element, altering DNA and granting resilience to Hoopers. The existence of “blanks” – humans with altered minds – suggests a darker side to the Polity and potential exploitation. Convocation is a formal gathering, and the Warden AI is a powerful entity with its own agenda. The Polity is expanding into Prador territory, and Cybercorp is interested in the technology and resources available.

## Tone & Atmosphere
The chapter is characterized by a gritty, unsettling atmosphere of alien danger, political tension, and ruthless pragmatism. Descriptions of violence, monstrous creatures, and the dehumanization of the blanks create a sense of unease and impending doom. The detached observation of the AI characters adds to the cold, calculating tone. The blend of the mundane and the bizarre contributes to a uniquely unsettling experience.
