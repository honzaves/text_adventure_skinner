# Chapter: Spatterjay 1 - The Skinner

## Events
The drones SM13 and Sniper conduct a marine census, encountering diverse lifeforms including glisters, turbul, whelks, boxies, prill, and even a packet-worm coral structure destroyed by Sniper. Simultaneously, Erlin and Janer observe a ship distinct from the *Treader*, prompting the ship to investigate. The *Treader* crew then harpoons and battles a massive leech, fending off swarms of prill that leap from its body. Janer and Erlin are welcomed aboard Captain Drum’s ship where they sample local cuisine – glister and hammer whelks – before Janer is attacked by a leech and rescued by Ron. Meanwhile, Keech arrives at a monolith covered in sail-like creatures, attempting to contact Erlin and Janer while being offered a trade by a Hive mind. On a Prador ship, Rebecca navigates a brutal society, battling lice and requesting a human “blank” from Ebulan, only to discover its intended use is for sexual exploitation.

## Characters Present
- **SM13:** Diligent drone, somewhat exasperated.
- **Sniper:** Cynical, destructive drone with a complex past.
- **Erlin:** Observant, knowledgeable about the Polity, with hidden motives.
- **Janer:** Curious, connected to a Hive mind, easily disturbed.
- **Captain Ron:** Strong, pragmatic rescuer.
- **Captain Drum:** Welcoming ship captain.
- **Rebecca (Frisk):** Pragmatic observer aboard a Prador ship.
- **Ebulan:** Powerful Prador, calculating and exploitative.
- **Vrell:** Adolescent Prador, curious about Rebecca’s request.
- **The Warden:** Assigns tasks, interested in environmental impact.
- **Sail:** Sentient creatures that control the ship’s sails.
- **Ambel:** Captain of the *Treader*, skilled fighter.
- **Pland:** Helmsman, assists with harpooning.

## Locations
- **Atoll:** Resembling an apple core, site of the initial census.
- **Sea:** Jade-colored, fractured by wakes, containing sargassum.
- **Ship (various):** Two-masted vessels, including the *Treader* and Captain Drum’s ship.
- **Monolith (“Big Flint”):** A flat-topped, black stone formation.
- **Prador Ship:** Dark, smelly, infested with lice.
- **Ebulan’s Chamber:** Lit with screens, smelling of decay.

## Creatures & Organisms
- **Glisters:** Rapacious marine predators, immune to a virus, harvested for meat.
- **Turbul:** Large fish, prey for glisters.
- **Whelks:** Food source for glisters, including aggressive hammer whelks.
- **Boxies:** Shoal creatures that feed on scraps.
- **Prill:** Small creatures that cling to leeches and leap onto decks.
- **Leeches:** Giant, parasitic creatures covered in prill.
- **Sea Leviathans:** Large creatures creating wakes.
- **Sails:** Large, sentient creatures with spined wings.
- **Prador:** Stratified alien species with carapaces and claws.
- **Teardrop Lice:** Infest the Prador ship.

## Items & Technology
- **Scanning probes:** Used for cataloging marine life.
- **Explosives:** Used by Sniper to destroy coral.
- **Image-intensifier:** Device with auto-tracking lenses.
- **QC laser:** Weapon carried by Janer.
- **Harpoons:** Used to capture leeches.
- **Blunderbuss:** Ambel’s weapon.
- **Pulse-gun:** Used by Keech.
- **Scooter:** Used by Keech for travel.
- **Hive Link:** Connection to Janer’s Hive mind.
- **Drug patch:** Applied to Janer’s neck.
- **Library console & crystals:** Used by Rebecca for cataloguing.
- **Ceramal doors:** Case-hardened doors to Ebulan’s chamber.

## Lore & World Details
- **Line world:** The setting for the drones’ census.
- **Polity:** A peaceful region, viewed with disdain by Rebecca.
- **Out-Polity world:** Receives limited assistance from the Polity.
- **Hoopers:** A poor people on the Out-Polity world, affected by sprine.
- **Sprine:** A valuable poison to Hoopers.
- **Prador Society:** Stratified and brutal, utilizing “blanks” as servants.
- **Third Kingdom:** A location with which Ebulan has contact.
- **Subsumption:** A process drones can undergo to become part of the Warden.

## Tone & Atmosphere
The overall tone is detached, gritty, and unsettling. A clinical focus on the marine ecosystem contrasts with the brutal realities of the Prador society and the violent encounters with the leech. There’s a sense of mystery surrounding Erlin’s motives, Janer’s connection to the Hive mind, and the true nature of the Polity’s involvement in this world. The atmosphere is often alien and dangerous, blending technological elements with primitive, organic environments.
