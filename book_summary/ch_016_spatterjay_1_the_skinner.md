# Chapter: Spatterjay 1 - The Skinner

## Chapter Summary

## Events
A Prador heirodont is consumed by a giant leech as rider prills feast nearby. Vrell, an adolescent Prador, expresses anxiety about a dangerous mission to detonate a device ashore near the Convocation fleet. He and three blanks launch toward the shore, but one falls into the sea. Simultaneously, the Prador ship comes under attack, with laser fire and missiles exchanged. The Old Captain, Drum, reveals he was controlled by a thrall unit, destroys it, and sabotages the ship’s controls before taking up arms. Vrell reaches the shore and begins assessing the situation.

Meanwhile, Ebulan, a powerful Prador, experiences intense pain from control boxes linked to his nervous system and suspects betrayal. He kills two of his “second-children” after they question him and attempts to sever the control links with the help of his remaining offspring. 

On the damaged ship, Drum battles a sail (Winscasher) who saves him from a blank, then witnesses the destruction of the Ahab by a massive wave, spotting another ship, the Treader, flung inland. A group of humans – Janer, Peck, Ambel, and Ron – are injured during the flood and begin pursuing a creature known as the “Skinner.” 

Vrell engages in combat with Drum, sustaining heavy injuries and undergoing a rapid, painful transformation into an adult Prador. Sniper, a war drone, engages in a desperate aerial battle with Prador drones, ultimately sacrificing itself to destroy one before being critically wounded. The Warden, controlling the drones, orders reinforcements to Skinner’s Island and sends another drone to search for a Prador vessel. Ebulan dispatches a probe to investigate a fleet of ships, but it is destroyed, fueling his anger and prompting him to consider aggressive action to cover his involvement in the “coring trade.”

## Characters Present
- **Vrell:** Adolescent Prador, anxious, tasked with a dangerous mission, undergoes a painful transformation.
- **Drum:** Human, formerly controlled by a thrall unit, rebellious, sabotages the ship, and engages in combat with Vrell.
- **Winscasher:** A sentient sail, protective of the ship, speaks with difficulty, recognizes Drum.
- **Ebulan:** Powerful Prador, experiencing pain, controlling, paranoid, and involved in the “coring trade.”
- **Sniper:** A war drone, skilled in combat, individualistic, and ultimately sacrifices itself.
- **Warden:** A controlling entity, commands drones, and prioritizes strategic objectives.
- **Janer, Peck, Ambel, Ron:** Humans, injured and determined to pursue the “Skinner.”
- **Blanks:** Humanoids controlled by Prador, used as tools/thralls, appear expendable.
- **Second-children:** Adolescent Prador, offspring of Ebulan.

## Locations
- **Ship (Prador Vessel/Ahab):** Damaged, equipped with advanced technology, and the site of conflict.
- **Sea:** Surrounds the ship and the shore, location of aerial combat and the destructive wave.
- **Shore/Beach/Dingle:** Vrell’s destination, a wooded area prone to flooding, and the site of intense combat.
- **Skinner’s Island:** Destination for drones One and Two.
- **Trench:** Location where drone Twelve is sent to search.
- **Ebulan’s Chamber:** A control room with screens and control boxes.

## Creatures & Organisms
- **Heirodont:** Large creature consumed by a leech.
- **Leech:** Giant, predatory.
- **Rider Prills:** Small creatures that feed on the leech’s prey.
- **Prador:** A species with a carapace, undergoing transformation from adolescent to adult.
- **Sail:** A sentient, possibly bio-mechanical sail.
- **“Blanks”:** Limbless beings controlled by Prador minds.
- **Skinner:** Large, blue creature being tracked by the humans.

## Items & Technology
- **Detonation Device:** To be deployed by Vrell.
- **Thrall Unit:** Implanted in the Captain's neck, used to control individuals.
- **Weapon (Drum’s):** Fires violet fire, disintegrating organic matter.
- **QC Laser/Shrapnel Rail-gun:** Weapons used by Vrell.
- **APW (Anti-Personnel Weapon):** Used by Keech and Sniper.
- **Missiles/Rail-guns:** Used by drones and ships.
- **Shield Projector:** Deflects incoming fire.
- **Control Boxes:** Linked to Prador nervous system, used to control “blanks.”
- **Screens/Arrays:** Used for monitoring and control.
- **Probe:** Used for reconnaissance.
- **Fusion Engine:** Powers Sniper’s craft.

## Lore & World Details
- The Convocation fleet is a significant force.
- Prador society has a hierarchical structure with “fathers” and “second-children.”
- Prador adolescence is linked to the death of the father and pheromonal control of limb development.
- “Blanks” are beings with nervous systems but no brains, controlled by Prador minds.
- The “coring trade” involves humans and is a source of conflict.
- There is a history of conflict between humans and Prador.

## Tone & Atmosphere
The chapter is intensely violent, chaotic, and foreboding. Descriptions of brutal combat, physical transformation, and the cold calculations of powerful entities create a gritty, desperate, and unforgiving atmosphere. There's a growing sense of menace as Ebulan contemplates extreme measures, and a pervasive feeling of loss and sacrifice.
