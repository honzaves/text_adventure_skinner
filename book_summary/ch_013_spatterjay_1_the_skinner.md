# Chapter: Spatterjay 1 - The Skinner

## Events
A giant whelk ascends from the deep ocean, experiencing discomfort, while a crew grapples with the aftermath of a devastating attack. The Ahab is destroyed by a Prador railgun after being ambushed by the Cohorn, leaving Boris and Roach overboard. Boris survives, covered in leeches, and eventually boards the burning Cohorn to aid the injured Roach. Meanwhile, Janer awakens aboard another ship and learns of an impending attack by Rebecca Frisk and Batian mercenaries wielding Prador weaponry, prompting a desperate flight towards Skinner’s Island. Frisk, revealed as cruel and erratic, sabotages her ship before departing with the Prador and mercenaries, leaving Roach to a grim fate. Keech attempts to aid the fleeing crew with a hover scooter but is attacked and forced to crash-land. The remaining crew, led by Captains Ron and Ambel, manage to drag their ship, the *Treader*, onto the shores of the ominous Skinner’s Island, while a scouting party ventures inland to locate the mysterious “Skinner.” Keech, piloting his damaged scooter, struggles to survive in the sea. Information packages reveal a complex web of political intrigue within the Prador Kingdom, centering on a powerful Prador named Ebulan and his dealings with human “blanks.”

## Characters Present
**Boris:** A regretful crew member, injured in the attack, determined to help Roach. **Goss:** A crew leader, killed in action. **Roach:** Seasoned crew member, tortured by Frisk and left aboard the sinking Cohorn. **Captain Ron & Captain Ambel:** Exceptionally strong “Old Captains” who lead the crew. **Janer:** A newly made Hooper, skeptical and observant, carrying a QC laser carbine. **Keech:** A monitor, attacked while attempting to support the crew, piloting a damaged scooter. **Rebecca Frisk:** Cruel and erratic, sabotages the ship and tortures Roach. **Vrell:** A Prador in command of the Cohorn, dismissive of Frisk. **Ebulan:** A powerful Prador involved in illicit trade, losing influence and controlling brain-altered drones. **Erlin, Forlam, Pland, Anne, Sild, Peck:** Various crew members contributing to the unfolding events.

## Locations
**The Ocean:** Home to giant whelks, heirodonts, leeches, and the wreckage of the Ahab. **The Ahab:** Destroyed ship, site of the initial attack. **The Cohorn:** Burning ship, now controlled by Frisk and the Prador. **Skinner’s Island:** Rocky island with dense vegetation (“dingle”), shallows, and a sinister reputation. **The *Treader*:** Ship used to reach Skinner’s Island. **Prador Worlds & Third Kingdom:** Locations of political intrigue and conflict.

## Creatures & Organisms
**Whelk, Heirodont, Leeches, Molly Carp, Glisters:** Deep-sea creatures inhabiting the ocean. **Prador:** Armored alien species wielding advanced weaponry. **Frogmoles, Lung Birds, Putrephallus Weeds, Rhinoworms:** Dangerous creatures and plants inhabiting Skinner’s Island. **Iron Seahorse:** A mysterious creature rising from the sea.

## Items & Technology
**Deck Cannon, Pulsed-Energy Handgun, Pump-Action Shotguns, Railgun:** Weapons used in the attack. **Hive link, QC laser carbine, Translator box:** Communication and advanced weaponry. **Hover Scooter:** Damaged vehicle used for attack and evasion. **Ship *Treader*, Remote Probes, Thruster, AG (Anti-Gravity):** Various modes of transportation and surveillance. **Filter Mask, Machete, Blunderbuss:** Tools and weapons for survival.

## Lore & World Details
The world features deep-sea creatures, advanced technology, and political conflicts between humans and the Prador. “Old Captains” possess extraordinary strength. A network called “the Hive” exists, and Hoopers are individuals with extended lifespans. The Prador are involved in trade with the Polity, but unrest is brewing within their kingdom, particularly surrounding Ebulan’s dealings with human “blanks.”

## Tone & Atmosphere
The overall tone is tense, foreboding, and often grim. The narrative is filled with a sense of impending doom, violence, and desperation. The descriptions of the attack, the brutal deaths, and the dangerous environment create a dark and unsettling atmosphere, punctuated by moments of frantic preparation and grim acceptance. The mystery surrounding Skinner’s Island and the political intrigue within the Prador Kingdom add to the sense of unease and the unknown.
