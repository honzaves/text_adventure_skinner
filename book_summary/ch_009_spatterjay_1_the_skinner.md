# Chapter: Spatterjay 1 - The Skinner

## Events
A glister is consumed by a creature disguised as a boulder, and the crew of the *Treader* harvests a giant leech, facing complications from a molly carp and a near-fatal accident involving Pland. Simultaneously, Rebecca Frisk oversees the capture of Captain Drum, while Sable Keech is resurrected after 700 years, investigates a murder, and is seemingly killed by Alphed Rimsc only to awaken as a “reif.” Ambel and Boris discuss a past trauma involving a shooting, observed by the sentient sail Windcheater, who reports to the Warden. The Warden reveals Frisk’s arrival and the gathering of the Old Captains.

## Characters Present
- **Sable Keech:** Reanimated investigator, skilled hunter, driven by past conflicts, now a “reif.”
- **Alphed Rimsc:** Keech’s opponent, physically enhanced, responsible for Keech’s initial death.
- **Captain Ambel:** Ship captain, scarred, prone to violent outbursts, suffering from memory loss.
- **Boris:** Helmsman, also suffering from memory loss.
- **Rebecca Frisk:** Cold and calculating, oversees operations, her arrival signals a significant event.
- **Svan:** Batian mercenary, follows Frisk’s orders.
- **Janer & Erlin:** Provide medical care, with Erlin revealing an unexpectedly long lifespan.
- **Captain Drum:** Hooper Captain, restrained and captured.
- **Pland:** Crew member, prone to cursing, requires assistance after a near-fatal accident.
- **Peck:** Crew member, concerned for Ambel, attacked during Ambel’s outburst.
- **Windcheater:** Sentient sail, observant, sarcastic, reports to the Warden.
- **Sniper:** War drone, frustrated with watch duty.
- **Gollow & Sild:** Crew members assisting with medical care.

## Locations
- **Ship (Various):** The *Treader*, a sailing vessel navigating rough seas, and the Prador ship holding Captain Drum. Features cabins, decks, and a forecabin.
- **Sea:** Surrounding the ships, containing sargassum, submerged objects, molly carp, and leeches.
- **Island of Sargassum:** Used for camouflage by the Prador ship.
- **Cove:** The *Treader* departs from this location.
- **Erlin’s Drug Manufactory:** Located within a ship, connected to medical equipment.
- **Klader Streets:** Hydrocar-paved streets, often rainy.
- **Apartment:** A crime scene with a brutally murdered victim.
- **Tank:** Where Keech undergoes regeneration.
- **Olian Tay’s Island:** Windcheater’s destination.

## Creatures & Organisms
- **Glister:** Large creature with an “adamantine shell,” preyed upon by a disguised predator.
- **Spatterjay Leviathan:** A type of sea creature.
- **Hooper:** Strong and durable humanoids, requiring full-restraint harnesses.
- **Batians:** Heavy-worlders, physically strong.
- **Leech:** Giant, slimy creatures covered in prill, harvested for their internal organs.
- **Molly Carp:** Large, crafty fish that attacks the leech’s tail.
- **Prill:** Fragments that grow into more prill.

## Items & Technology
- **Autodoc:** Medical device used for treatment.
- **Ceramal Restraint Harness:** Used to restrain Captain Drum.
- **Pulse-gun:** A powerful weapon used by Prador attackers and Rimsc.
- **Thin-gun:** A small, concealed weapon used by Keech.
- **Harpoons, Knives, & Deck Cannon:** Used for hunting and securing leeches.
- **Injector:** Used by Frisk to counteract a reaction.
- **Aug:** Communication device used by Windcheater.
- **Sprine:** A rare substance, possibly a drug or medicine.
- **Sea-cane rum & Hammer whelks:** Consumables.
- **Vis gun & Memplant:** Technology related to memory manipulation.
- **Cybplant:** Something Keech exhibits physical resistance to.

## Lore & World Details
- The world contains various species, including glisters, Hoopers, and Batians.
- The Prador are a hostile force with advanced technology.
- ECS can alter genetic code.
- Hoopers can be taken as slaves (“coring”).
- There is a history of long-term “coring” of Hooper humans.
- People can be revived after death as “reifs.”
- There is a Warden overseeing security.
- The “Old Captains” are gathering, suggesting a significant event or conflict.
- A virus affects Hoopers.
- Erlin is over two centuries old but appears young.

## Tone & Atmosphere
The narrative is gritty, violent, and filled with a sense of underlying danger. It blends physical struggle with psychological tension, exploring themes of memory, death, and exploitation. The atmosphere is bleak and mysterious, hinting at a complex and dangerous world with a troubled history.
