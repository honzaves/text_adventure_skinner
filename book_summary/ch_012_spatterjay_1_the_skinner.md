# Chapter: Spatterjay 1 - The Skinner

## Events
A glister was injured and killed by molly carp while attempting to surface. Ebulan, a Prador councillor, brutally trains his son, Vrell, culminating in a dangerous mission. Ebulan is revealed to be orchestrating a complex plan involving an antimatter-powered “motor” and the capture of Keech and Frisk. Simultaneously, four individuals arrived via a “Skaidon warp” at a runcible monitored by the AI, the Warden. The Warden detected an unregistered antigravity device and suspected Prador involvement beyond Frisk’s arrival, dispatching agents SM12 and SM13 to investigate a sailing ship. Inside the damaged ship, *Treader*, Svan and Shib assessed the damage while Speaker revealed Frisk is experiencing psychosis due to medication withdrawal and poses a threat. Svan and Shib stunned Frisk, and Speaker disclosed political motivations for subduing her – severing ties with cored human trade and eliminating witnesses to past actions. Shib violently abused the unconscious Frisk before being stopped by Svan. A camouflaged seahorse appeared on the mast, and the ship departed the atolls for Deep-sea. Keech discussed the dangerous Skinner with Forlam, expressing concern about the upcoming Convocation. 

SM13, observing the ship, detected Prador glyphs on a one-armed woman before being destroyed by an EM burst. Shib shot down the drone, then was attacked by a Prador who boarded with human blanks, ordering a course change. Svan realized they had been deceived when she saw a blank swapping injectors on Frisk. The Warden confirmed Rebecca Frisk’s arrival was a cover and linked events to a Prador presence. Svan and Shib watched as blanks armed the deck after their transport ship sank. The Warden received a message and EM blast, confirming an adult Prador’s presence on the planet and directed drones to investigate. Ambel and Ron discussed sleeping arrangements, with Ambel contemplating violence but ultimately dismissing it. Ambel and Erlin discussed pain, betrayal, and finding meaning in a long life, with Erlin admitting her need for guidance. They made a pact: Ambel would help Erlin live if the “judgement” favored him, and Erlin would retrieve sprine crystals for Ambel if it didn’t. Erlin left to fulfill a task, and Ambel experienced a cool breeze and the taste of sea spray.

## Lore & World Details
- **Prador:** A species that initiates and manipulates, utilizing “blanks” (controlled human bodies).
- **Convocation:** A gathering of Old Captains, Keech, and Frisk.
- **Blank:** A human body controlled by the Prador.
- **Batians:** A species represented by Svan and Shib.
- **Golems:** Androids.
- **Old Captains:** Long-lived individuals (some centuries old) who have achieved a state of calm. Suicide is common before the three-century mark.
- **Cored Humans:** A practice involving the removal of something from humans.
- **Skinner (Jay Hoop):** A dangerous individual who inflicted severe pain on others.
- **Polity:** A kingdom with growing ties to the Prador Kingdom.

## Tone & Atmosphere
The narrative is tense, foreboding, and filled with manipulation. There's a sense of complex plotting, hidden agendas, and a cold, calculating atmosphere surrounding the Prador characters. A melancholic undercurrent explores themes of regret, purpose, and the weight of a long life, particularly in the conversations between Ambel and Erlin. The overall atmosphere is one of unease and impending conflict.
