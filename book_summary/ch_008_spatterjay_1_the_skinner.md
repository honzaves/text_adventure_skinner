# Chapter: Spatterjay 1 - The Skinner

## Events
The crew of a ship navigates waters filled with scavenging prill and leeches, hampered by poor visibility. Keech, a severely injured man exhibiting a mix of organic and cybernetic components and infected with the Spatterjay virus, arrives via AG scooter. Erlin diagnoses him and begins treatment with a nano-changer and Intertox, requiring Janer to construct a makeshift tank using a monofilament mainsail. Simultaneously, Frisk interrogates four mercenaries – Tay, Svan, Shib, and Duon & Eon Talsca (mentioned in flashback) – about their knowledge of Skinner’s Island, with Tay fabricating a story about paintings by an artist named Sprage. Tay leads Frisk to a hidden chamber containing the artwork before being subdued. Ambel and his crew return to the ship with sacks of blood from a hunt, discussing future pursuits. Windcheater, an intelligent sail creature, negotiates wages with Sniper for work on the Treader, researching Hooper ship contracts. Tay recovers from her injuries, revealing her Hooper physiology and the strength of the “Prador armour” protecting her safe, and revives David Grenant from a coffin-like stasis. Frisk and a drone, SM Twelve, arrive at Tay’s wrecked home. The Warden, communicating through the drone, reveals Frisk has body-swapped and mind-wiped her original self. The Warden and Tay discuss Frisk’s arrival, with the drone relaying potential motives: to kill Keech, find her husband, or simply *be* there. Tay observes men glaring at her tower and learns Sprage is coming to visit.

## Lore & World Details
- **Spatterjay Virus:** Digests dead tissue and affects both living and dead creatures.
- **Hoopers:** A species with a unique attitude towards technology, possessing rapid healing, and functioning as sails on ships.
- **Reifs:** A term for individuals, possibly a species.
- **Hive Mind:** An entity communicating through a hornet.
- **The Dome:** A possible safe haven.
- **Prador:** An ancient civilization involved in a long war.
- **Old Captains:** A group including Sprage.
- **Polity:** A civilization with citizens who might believe the Warden’s explanations.
- **Body-swapping & Mind-wiping:** Advanced technologies.

## Tone & Atmosphere
The overall tone is grim, unsettling, and suspenseful. Descriptions of decay, a desperate medical situation, and the threat of violence create a sense of unease and mystery. The world is depicted as harsh and dangerous, with a blend of technological sophistication and brutal practicality. There’s a constant undercurrent of surveillance and intrigue, as characters are observed and manipulated by unseen forces.
